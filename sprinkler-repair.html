Any of these sound familiar?

Eight patterns cover 95% of repair calls we run. Click any that match — or note them down and the AI intake will ask.

💧

No water at the heads

Zone runs but no spray, or all zones are dry. Usually a closed valve, controller fault, or pressure-supply problem.

📉

Low pressure across zones

Heads barely pop up, weak spray pattern. Often a pressure regulator, partially-closed gate valve, or split mainline.

🌊

Wet spots that won't dry

Soggy patch on the lawn, water surfacing between zone runs. Usually a buried leak — a split poly fitting or cracked irrigation pipe.

🟫

Dry spots / brown patches

Zone runs but coverage gaps. Could be a head buried by thatch, broken rotor, blocked nozzle, or wrong-arc nozzle for the area.

🔌

Controller won't program

Clock loss after a power blip, Hydrawise app won't connect, schedule won't save, zones running at wrong times.

⚠️

Zone won't shut off

Zone keeps running after the schedule should end. Almost always a stuck valve solenoid or debris in the diaphragm.

🔨

Broken / leaning heads

Snowplow damage in spring, lawn-mower or shovel impact during the season. Heads are cheap; the labour to swap them is the variable.

❄️

Freeze damage from winter

Cracked manifold, split poly, blown valve diaphragm. Common on systems that weren't professionally winterized last fall.

Save the call-out fee. Here's how.

We'd rather you save your money where you can. Two things you can usually DIY safely. Five things that'll cost you more if you try.

✓ DIY-safe (save the call-out fee)

  • Replace a broken spray head — unscrew the broken one, hand-tighten a matching new one from any irrigation supply ($5–$15 part). Don't over-tighten.
  • Reset your controller after a power loss — re-enter your programming, or in the Hydrawise app reconnect WiFi and restore your saved schedule.

⚠ Call us — DIY usually costs more

  • Wet-spot leak hunting — wrong-zone digging is how single-fitting repairs become full landscaping rebuilds.
  • Low pressure across zones — could be 6 different things; misdiagnosis costs $200+ in wrong parts before you find it.
  • Stuck or leaking valves — diaphragms tear easily and the wrong replacement creates new leaks.
  • Anything with the mainline — high-pressure water + buried irrigation pipe + amateur fittings = bigger leaks.
  • Pressure regulator failures — wrong PSI ruins every nozzle on the system over time.
